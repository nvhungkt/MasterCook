<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-family: Helvetica, sans-serif; background-image: initial; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">1 tablespoon active dry yeast <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-family: Helvetica, sans-serif; background-image: initial; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">1 teaspoon white sugar <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-family: Helvetica, sans-serif; background-image: initial; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">2 tablespoons butter, softened <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-family: Helvetica, sans-serif; background-image: initial; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">1 1/3 cups water <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-family: Helvetica, sans-serif; background-image: initial; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">1/4 teaspoon salt <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-family: Helvetica, sans-serif; background-image: initial; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">3 tablespoons baking soda <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-family: Helvetica, sans-serif; background-image: initial; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">3 cups water <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-family: Helvetica, sans-serif; background-image: initial; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">2 tablespoons butter, melted <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-family: Helvetica, sans-serif; background-image: initial; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">1 tablespoon coarse sea salt, or to taste<o:p></o:p></span></p><p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-family: Helvetica, sans-serif; background-image: initial; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;"></span></p><h2 class="responsive-tabs__pane-title" style="margin: 0px 0px 15px; font-family: SerifaBT-Light, Georgia, "Times New Roman", Times, serif; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); text-rendering: optimizeLegibility; font-size: 25px; padding-top: 20px; padding-bottom: 10px; background-image: url("/sites/all/themes/bbcw_goodfood/images/ui/border-dash.3.gif?vcea4046353d3aff5c44a4d7f021d9f80") !important; background-position: left bottom !important; background-size: initial !important; background-repeat: repeat-x !important; background-attachment: initial !important; background-origin: initial !important; background-clip: initial !important;">Method</h2></div><div><p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-family: Helvetica, sans-serif; background-image: initial; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">1. In a large bowl, stir together 1 cup of flour, yeast, sugar,
2 tablespoons of butter, and 1 1/3 cup water. Let this mixture stand until
bubble begin to form, about 15 minutes. Stir in the salt and gradually stir in
the remaining flour until dough can be picked up and kneaded on the counter.
Knead until smooth and elastic, about 8 minutes, adding more flour if needed.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-family: Helvetica, sans-serif; background-image: initial; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">2. Divide the dough into 6 pieces and let them rest for a few
minutes. Roll out one piece at a time into a rope about 15 inches long. Loop
and twist into that cool pretzel shape. Set on a baking sheet while you roll
out the remaining portions.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-family: Helvetica, sans-serif; background-image: initial; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">3. Preheat the oven to 450 degrees F (220 degrees C). Bring the
remaining 3 cups of water to a boil and add the baking soda. Remove from the
heat. Dip pretzels into the water bath for about 45 seconds, flipping over
about halfway through. Place the soaked pretzels on a greased baking sheet.
Brush them with melted butter and sprinkle with coarse salt.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-family: Helvetica, sans-serif; background-image: initial; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">4. Bake in the preheated oven until golden brown, 8 to 10 minutes.<o:p></o:p></span></p></div>
