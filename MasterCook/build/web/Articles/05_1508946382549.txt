<img src="Pictures/05_1508946127671.jpg"><div><h2 class="responsive-tabs__pane-title" style="margin: 0px 0px 15px; font-family: SerifaBT-Light, Georgia, "Times New Roman", Times, serif; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); text-rendering: optimizeLegibility; font-size: 26px; padding-top: 20px; padding-bottom: 10px; background-image: url("/sites/all/themes/bbcw_goodfood/images/ui/border-dash.3.gif?vcea4046353d3aff5c44a4d7f021d9f80") !important; background-position: left bottom !important; background-size: initial !important; background-repeat: repeat-x !important; background-attachment: initial !important; background-origin: initial !important; background-clip: initial !important;">Ingredients</h2></div><div><p class="MsoNormal"><b>For the crust</b><o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">85g butter melted, plus extra for tin<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">140g digestive biscuit, made into fine crumbs<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">1 tbsp sugar, granulated or golden caster<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b>For the cheesecake filling<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">3 x 300g/11oz pack Philadelphia cheese, or other full-fat
soft cheese<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">250g golden caster sugar<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">3 tbsp plain flour<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">1½ tsp vanilla extract<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">finely grated zest of 1 lemon (about 2 tsp)<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">1½ tsp lemon juice<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">3 large eggs, plus 1 yolk<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">284ml carton soured cream<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b>For the soured cream topping<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">142ml carton soured cream<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">1 tbsp golden caster sugar<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">2 tsp lemon juice<o:p></o:p></p><h2 class="responsive-tabs__pane-title" style="margin: 0px 0px 15px; font-family: SerifaBT-Light, Georgia, "Times New Roman", Times, serif; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); text-rendering: optimizeLegibility; font-size: 26px; padding-top: 20px; padding-bottom: 10px; background-image: url("/sites/all/themes/bbcw_goodfood/images/ui/border-dash.3.gif?vcea4046353d3aff5c44a4d7f021d9f80") !important; background-position: left bottom !important; background-size: initial !important; background-repeat: repeat-x !important; background-attachment: initial !important; background-origin: initial !important; background-clip: initial !important;">Method</h2></div><div><ol class="method__list" style="margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; margin-left: 0px; padding: 0px; list-style: none; font-family: "Helvetica Neue", Helvetica, Arial; font-size: 15px;"><li class="method__item" itemprop="recipeInstructions" style="line-height: 1.6; font-size: 16px; border-bottom: 1px solid rgb(240, 242, 235); counter-increment: method 1; margin-bottom: 20px; padding-bottom: 20px;"><p style="line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 2px;">1. Position an oven shelf in the middle of the oven. Preheat the oven to fan 160C/conventional 180C/gas 4. Line the base of a 23cm springform <a href="https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/howto/guide/baking-equipment" style="color: rgb(68, 125, 117); cursor: pointer;">cake tin</a> with parchment paper. For the crust, melt the butter in a medium <a href="https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/content/five-best-saucepans" style="color: rgb(68, 125, 117); cursor: pointer;">pan</a>. Stir in the biscuit crumbs and sugar so the mixture is evenly moistened. Press the mixture into the bottom of the pan and bake for 10 minutes. Cool on a <a href="https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/content/kimberley-wilsons-top-five-cooling-racks" style="color: rgb(68, 125, 117); cursor: pointer;">wire rack</a> while preparing the filling.</p></li><li class="method__item" itemprop="recipeInstructions" style="line-height: 1.6; font-size: 16px; border-bottom: 1px solid rgb(240, 242, 235); counter-increment: method 1; margin-bottom: 20px; padding-bottom: 20px;"><p style="line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 2px;">2. For the filling, increase the oven temperature to fan 200C/conventional 240C/gas 9. In a <a href="https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/content/test-five-best-food-processors" style="color: rgb(68, 125, 117); cursor: pointer;">table top mixer</a> fitted with the paddle attachment, beat the soft cheese at medium-low speed until creamy, about 2 minutes. With the mixer on low, gradually add the sugar, then the flour and a pinch of salt, scraping down the sides of the bowl and the paddle twice.</p></li><li class="method__item" itemprop="recipeInstructions" style="line-height: 1.6; font-size: 16px; border-bottom: 1px solid rgb(240, 242, 235); counter-increment: method 1; margin-bottom: 20px; padding-bottom: 20px;"><p style="line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 2px;">3. Swap the paddle attachment for the whisk. Continue by adding the vanilla, lemon zest and juice. Whisk in the eggs and yolk, one at a time, scraping the bowl and whisk at least twice. Stir the 284ml carton of soured cream until smooth, then measure 200ml/7fl oz (just over 3?4 of the carton). Continue on low speed as you add the measured soured cream (reserve the rest). Whisk to blend, but don't over-beat. The batter should be smooth, light and somewhat airy.</p></li><li class="method__item" itemprop="recipeInstructions" style="line-height: 1.6; font-size: 16px; border-bottom: 1px solid rgb(240, 242, 235); counter-increment: method 1; margin-bottom: 20px; padding-bottom: 20px;"><p style="line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 2px;">4. Brush the sides of the springform tin with melted butter and put on a baking sheet. Pour in the filling - if there are any lumps, sink them using a knife - the top should be as smooth as possible. Bake for 10 minutes. Reduce oven temperature to fan 90C/conventional 110C/gas 1?4 and bake for 25 minutes more. If you gently shake the tin, the filling should have a slight wobble. Turn off the oven and open the oven door for a cheesecake that's creamy in the centre, or leave it closed if you prefer a drier texture. Let cool in the oven for 2 hours. The cheesecake may get a slight crack on top as it cools.</p></li><li class="method__item" itemprop="recipeInstructions" style="line-height: 1.6; font-size: 16px; border-bottom: 1px solid rgb(240, 242, 235); counter-increment: method 1; margin-bottom: 20px; padding-bottom: 20px;"><p style="line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 2px;">5. Combine the reserved soured cream with the 142ml carton, the sugar and lemon juice for the topping. Spread over the cheesecake right to the edges. Cover loosely with foil and refrigerate for at least 8 hours or overnight.</p></li><li class="method__item" itemprop="recipeInstructions" style="line-height: 1.6; font-size: 16px; border-bottom: 1px solid rgb(240, 242, 235); counter-increment: method 1; padding-bottom: 20px; margin-bottom: 0px !important;"><p style="line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 2px;">6. Run a round-bladed knife around the sides of the tin to loosen any stuck edges. Unlock the side, slide the cheesecake off the bottom of the tin onto a plate, then slide the parchment paper out from underneath.</p></li></ol></div>
