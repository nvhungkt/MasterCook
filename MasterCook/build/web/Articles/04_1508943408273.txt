<p class="MsoNormal">½ tsp vanilla extract<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">250g plain flour, from a new bag.<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">1 tsp baking powder<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">about 1 litre sunflower oil<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">few chunks of bread<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b>For the sauce</b><o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">200g bar dark chocolate, not too bitter, broken into chunks<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">100ml double cream<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">100ml whole milk<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">3 tbsp golden syrup<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">½ tsp vanilla extract, if you have some<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b>For the cinnamon sugar</b><o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">100g caster sugar<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">2 tsp cinnamon<o:p></o:p></p><h2 class="responsive-tabs__pane-title" style="margin: 0px 0px 15px; font-family: SerifaBT-Light, Georgia, "Times New Roman", Times, serif; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); text-rendering: optimizeLegibility; font-size: 25px; padding-top: 20px; padding-bottom: 10px; background-image: url("/sites/all/themes/bbcw_goodfood/images/ui/border-dash.3.gif?vcea4046353d3aff5c44a4d7f021d9f80") !important; background-position: left bottom !important; background-size: initial !important; background-repeat: repeat-x !important; background-attachment: initial !important; background-origin: initial !important; background-clip: initial !important;">Method</h2></div><div><ol class="method__list" style="margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; margin-left: 0px; padding: 0px; list-style: none; font-family: "Helvetica Neue", Helvetica, Arial; font-size: 15px;"><li class="method__item" itemprop="recipeInstructions" style="line-height: 1.6; font-size: 16px; border-bottom: 1px solid rgb(240, 242, 235); counter-increment: method 1; margin-bottom: 20px; padding-bottom: 20px;"><p style="line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 2px;">1. Boil the kettle, then measure 350ml boiling water into a jug and add the melted butter and vanilla extract. Sift the flour and baking powder into a big mixing bowl with a big pinch of salt. Make a well in the centre, then pour in the contents of the jug and very quickly beat into the flour with a wooden spoon until lump-free. Rest for 10-15 mins while you make the sauce.</p></li><li class="method__item" itemprop="recipeInstructions" style="line-height: 1.6; font-size: 16px; border-bottom: 1px solid rgb(240, 242, 235); counter-increment: method 1; margin-bottom: 20px; padding-bottom: 20px;"><p style="line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 2px;">2. Put all the sauce ingredients into a pan and gently melt together, stirring occasionally until you have a smooth shiny sauce. Keep warm on a low heat.</p></li><li class="method__item" itemprop="recipeInstructions" style="line-height: 1.6; font-size: 16px; border-bottom: 1px solid rgb(240, 242, 235); counter-increment: method 1; margin-bottom: 20px; padding-bottom: 20px;"><p style="line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 2px;">3. Fill a large deep saucepan one-third full of oil. Cooking with hot oil can be dangerous, read up on how to deep-fry safely to avoid accidents in the kitchen. Heat until a cube of bread browns in 45 seconds – 1 min. Cover a tray with kitchen paper and mix the caster sugar and cinnamon together.</p></li><li class="method__item" itemprop="recipeInstructions" style="line-height: 1.6; font-size: 16px; border-bottom: 1px solid rgb(240, 242, 235); counter-increment: method 1; padding-bottom: 20px; margin-bottom: 0px !important;"><p style="line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 2px;">4. Fit a star nozzle to a piping bag, 1.5-2cm wide is a good size. Fill with the rested dough, then pipe 2-3 strips directly into the pan, snipping off each dough strip with a pair of kitchen scissors. Fry until golden brown and crisp. Be very careful here as if air bubbles form in the churros they can explode - keep children out of the kitchen and protect yourself by wearing long sleeves, eye protection and keeping your face away from the pan. then remove with a slotted spoon and drain on the kitchen paper-lined tray. Carry on cooking the rest of the dough in batches, sprinkling the cooked churros with some cinnamon sugar as you go. Once you’ve cooked all the churros, toss with any remaining cinnamon sugar and serve with the chocolate sauce, for dipping. <em>It's important to take care when cooking with hot oil - read our guide on <span style="font-style: normal;">how to deep-fry safely </span>to avoid accidents in the kitchen.</em></p></li></ol></div>
