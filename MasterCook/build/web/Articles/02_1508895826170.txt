font-size: 15px;"><li class="method__item" itemprop="recipeInstructions" style="line-height: 1.6; font-size: 16px; border-bottom: 1px solid rgb(240, 242, 235); counter-increment: method 1; margin-bottom: 20px; padding-bottom: 20px;"><p style="line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 2px;">1. Make the base: Put the flour into a large bowl, then stir in the yeast and salt. Make a well, pour in 200ml warm water and the olive oil and bring together with a wooden spoon until you have a soft, fairly wet dough. Turn onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 5 mins until smooth. Cover with a tea towel and set aside. You can leave the dough to rise if you like, but it’s not essential for a thin crust.</p></li><li class="method__item" itemprop="recipeInstructions" style="line-height: 1.6; font-size: 16px; border-bottom: 1px solid rgb(240, 242, 235); counter-increment: method 1; margin-bottom: 20px; padding-bottom: 20px;"><p style="line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 2px;">2. Make the sauce: Mix the passata, basil and crushed garlic together, then season to taste. Leave to stand at room temperature while you get on with shaping the base.</p></li><li class="method__item" itemprop="recipeInstructions" style="line-height: 1.6; font-size: 16px; border-bottom: 1px solid rgb(240, 242, 235); counter-increment: method 1; margin-bottom: 20px; padding-bottom: 20px;"><p style="line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 2px;">3. Roll out the dough: If you’ve let the dough rise, give it a quick knead, then split into two balls. On a floured surface, roll out the dough into large rounds, about 25cm across, using a rolling pin. The dough needs to be very thin as it will rise in the oven. Lift the rounds onto two floured <a href="https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/content/kimberley-wilsons-top-5-baking-trays" style="color: rgb(68, 125, 117); cursor: pointer;">b</a>aking sheets.</p></li><li class="method__item" itemprop="recipeInstructions" style="line-height: 1.6; font-size: 16px; border-bottom: 1px solid rgb(240, 242, 235); counter-increment: method 1; padding-bottom: 20px; margin-bottom: 0px !important;"><p style="line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 2px;">4. Top and bake: Heat oven to 240C/fan 220C /gas 8. Put another baking sheet or an upturned baking tray in the oven on the top shelf. Smooth sauce over bases with the back of a spoon. Scatter with cheese and tomatoes, drizzle with olive oil and season. Put one pizza, still on its baking sheet, on top of the preheated sheet or tray. Bake for 8-10 mins until crisp. Serve with a little more olive oil, and basil leaves if using. Repeat step for remaining pizza.</p></li></ol></div><div><br></div>
