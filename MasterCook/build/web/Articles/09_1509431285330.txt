<p class="MsoNormal">1 small red chilli (deseeded if you don't like it too hot),

<p class="MsoNormal">1 tbsp finely chopped ginger<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">1 tbsp golden caster sugar<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">3 tbsp fish sauce<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">juice 1 lime<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b>For the rolls</b><o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">100g vermicelli rice noodle or bean thread noodles<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">12 x 20cm round rice<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"> paper wrappers (see

<p class="MsoNormal">1 carrot, cut into thin batons</p><p class="MsoNormal"><o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">a handful coriander<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">a handful Thai basil<o:p></o:p></p>

<p class="MsoNormal">a handful long chives<o:p></o:p></p>

Alternatively, just blitz everything together in a mini blender.</p></li><li class="method__item" itemprop="recipeInstructions" style="line-height: 1.6; font-size: 16px; border-bottom: 1px solid rgb(240, 242, 235); counter-increment: method 1; margin-bottom: 20px; padding-bottom: 20px;"><p style="line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 2px;">2. When you’re about halfway, fold the ends of the rice paper in and over the filling so that it is completely enclosed.</p></li><li class="method__item" itemprop="recipeInstructions" style="line-height: 1.6; font-size: 16px; border-bottom: 1px solid rgb(240, 242, 235); counter-increment: method 1; padding-bottom: 20px; margin-bottom: 0px !important;"><p style="line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 2px;">8. Keep on rolling tightly until the whole rice paper wrapper is rolled up. To serve, cut the rolls in half on the diagonal.</p></li></ol></div>
